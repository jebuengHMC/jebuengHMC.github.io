About my hearing in the workplace

Hello! Thanks for clicking this link 👋🏽

This is Jonathan from CIS. For those who haven't met me, I'm part of the User Support Services team and provide IT support👨🏽‍💻 for the college.

My hearing

  • In June 2022 I lost most of my hearing in both ears👂🏽.
  • My right ear is still able to hear although is very limited.
  • I am currently unable to get a hearing aid just yet but I am planning to.
  • It’s difficult for me to understand speech in-person🗣 or over the phone📞.
  • I am using some assistive technology such as speech-to-text apps📱 and the iPhone’s Live Listen feature to help with my hearing, but I may still encounter difficulty verbally communicating with you.
  • I am generally able to hear you if I'm right next to you or if you face me👁 and project📢 your voice louder than you usually do when farther away.
  • I can hear reasonably well over the phone📞 but occasionally miss some words.

Tips for communicating with me

  • If you need my attention, tap me👇🏽 on the shoulder or make eye contact👁 and wave👋🏽 instead of calling my name🗣🚫.
  • I may ask you to repeat yourself several times
  • If I am using a speech-to-text app, there might be a delay before I understand what you are saying.
  • Chat👨🏽‍💻 or email📧 is preferred.
  • Talking one-on-one is preferred, but if I’m in a group conversation, I might not pick up what everyone is saying without the help of voice transcription technology.

Please feel free to email me for any questions, comments, or resources that you think would be helpful for me or the community.
